# Aircraft Parts Demand Forecasting System
## **Executive Summary**

**Purpose:** Predict quarterly demand for 10,000+ aircraft parts up to 2 years in advance to optimize inventory levels, reduce stockouts, and minimize excess inventory costs.

**Business Value:**
- 🎯 **Automated Planning**: Forecasts for all parts without manual intervention
- 💰 **Cost Reduction**: Optimal inventory levels reduce carrying costs and emergency orders
- ⚠️ **Risk Mitigation**: Early alerts for demand spikes and trend changes
- 🔧 **Maintenance-Aware**: Adjusts forecasts during planned aircraft downtime
- 📊 **Intelligent Classification**: Tells you which parts to trust vs. which need manual review

**What Makes This System Reliable:**
This system uses **4 layers of quality control** to ensure forecasts are realistic and actionable:
1. **Smart Model Selection** - Automatically chooses the right forecasting method based on each part's history
2. **Reality Checks** - Rejects unrealistic forecasts (e.g., predicting 10x demand spikes)
3. **Multiple Expert Opinions** - Combines forecasts from multiple models when they agree
4. **Safety Minimums** - Applies business rules to prevent forecasts that are too low

---

## **How It Works: 4-Step Quality Control Process**

### **Step 1: Data Quality Assessment** 📋

Before forecasting, the system evaluates each part's historical demand pattern:

| Pattern Type | What It Means | Example | How System Responds |
|-------------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Healthy** | Consistent orders, 2+ years of history | Ordered regularly, stable quantities | Uses all 9 forecasting methods |
| **Intermittent** | Ordered sporadically (many zero months) | Order 50 units, then nothing for 6 months | Uses specialized "lumpy demand" methods |
| **Volatile** | Highly unpredictable quantities | Order 10, then 150, then 5 | Adds stabilization (blends with average) |
| **Sparse** | New part or limited history (<1 year) | Only 2-3 quarters of data | Uses simple averages only |

**Why This Matters:** Different demand patterns require different forecasting approaches. A method that works for consistent parts may fail for sporadic parts.

---

### **Step 2: Model Competition** 🏆

For each part, up to 9 different forecasting methods compete to generate the most accurate forecast:

**Simple Methods** (Always Used):
- **Moving Average**: Average of last 4 quarters
- **Trend Analysis**: Detects if demand is growing or declining
- **Recent Pattern**: Projects the most recent quarter forward

**Advanced Methods** (Used for Healthy Parts):
- **Seasonal Analysis**: Detects yearly patterns (e.g., higher demand in Q1)
- **Event-Aware Models**: Factors in planned maintenance schedules
- **Machine Learning**: Learns complex patterns from historical data

**Specialized Methods** (Used for Intermittent Parts):
- **Croston's Method**: Specifically designed for sporadic ordering patterns

**How We Pick the Winner:**
- Each method forecasts the last 6 months of known history
- System compares predictions to actual sales
- The most accurate method wins (lowest prediction error)

**Example:**
```
Part ABC123 - Historical: [520, 530, 525, 548]
├─ Moving Average predicts: 531 → Actual was 548 → Error: 17 units
├─ Trend Analysis predicts: 555 → Actual was 548 → Error: 7 units ✓ WINNER
└─ Recent Pattern predicts: 525 → Actual was 548 → Error: 23 units
Result: Use Trend Analysis for this part
```

---

### **Step 3: Reality Checks (Quality Gates)** 🚨

Even if a method has the lowest error, the system rejects it if the forecast is unrealistic:

**5 Automatic Checks:**

1. **Extreme Forecast Check**
   - ❌ Rejects: Forecasts >3x or <20% of your typical demand
   - Example: If you normally order 100 units/quarter, reject forecasts of 400+ or <20

2. **Volatility Check**
   - ❌ Rejects: Forecasts that fluctuate wildly (more than historical pattern)
   - Example: If your demand varies by ±50 units, reject forecasts varying by ±200 units

3. **Accuracy Check**
   - ❌ Rejects: Methods that were wrong by more than 100% during testing
   - Example: If average demand is 50 units, reject methods with 60+ unit errors

4. **Growth/Decline Check**
   - ❌ Rejects: Forecasts predicting decline when part is clearly growing (or vice versa)
   - Example: If last 4 quarters show 20%+ growth, reject forecasts showing decline

5. **Zero Collapse Check**
   - ❌ Rejects: Forecasts predicting near-zero demand for active parts
   - Example: If you've been ordering regularly, reject forecasts of 0-2 units

**What Happens if All Methods Fail These Checks?**
- System uses your historical average as the forecast
- Flags the part as "Low Confidence - Review Required"
- You'll see "Fallback (All Gated)" in the output

**Example:**
```
Part DEF456 - 8 Methods Tested:
✓ Trend Analysis (Error: 12 units) - PASSED all checks
✓ Moving Average (Error: 18 units) - PASSED all checks
✗ Recent Pattern (Predicted 850 vs typical 200) - FAILED Extreme Forecast Check
✗ Machine Learning (Predicted 5 vs typical 200) - FAILED Zero Collapse Check

Winner: Trend Analysis (most accurate + passed reality checks)
```

---

### **Step 4: Final Safety Adjustments** 🛡️

After selecting the best method, the system applies two final protections:

#### **A) Multiple Expert Opinions (When Appropriate)**

If 2-3 methods are **similarly accurate** (within 15% error), the system **combines them** instead of picking just one:

**Why Combine?**
- Reduces risk of relying on a single method
- Smooths out outlier predictions
- More stable forecasts

**How It Works:**
- More accurate methods get more weight in the blend
- Less accurate methods get less weight

**Example:**
```
Part GHI789 - Top 3 Methods:
├─ Trend Analysis: Error = 12 units (45% weight)
├─ Machine Learning: Error = 14 units (38% weight)
└─ Moving Average: Error = 16 units (17% weight)

Combined Forecast: (Trend×0.45) + (ML×0.38) + (MA×0.17)
Result: More reliable than any single method
```

#### **B) Forecast Minimums (Floor Protection)**

Even the best forecast can occasionally predict unrealistically low numbers. The system applies **4 safety minimums**:

1. **Historical Minimum**: Never forecast below 20% of your lowest non-zero order
2. **Recent Average**: Never forecast below 30% of last year's average
3. **Intermittent Floor**: For sporadic parts, minimum 10% of typical order size
4. **Absolute Minimum**: For active parts, always forecast at least 1 unit

**Why This Matters:**
- Prevents stockouts from over-optimistic forecasts
- Protects against forecasts that ignore known demand
- Ensures active parts stay in stock

**Example:**
```
Part JKL012:
├─ Winning Method Predicts: 3 units for Q1
├─ Historical Minimum (20% of 50): 10 units
├─ Recent Average Minimum (30% of 40): 12 units
└─ Final Forecast: 12 units (floor applied)

Output shows: "25Q1: 12 (floor)" to indicate safety adjustment
```

## **Understanding Your Forecast Output**

### **Which Parts Can You Trust?** 🎯

The system automatically classifies every part into **4 Forecastability Tiers**. This tells you how to use each forecast:

---

#### **Tier 1: HIGH CONFIDENCE** ✅ (Green Light - Trust These)

**What It Means:**
- 2+ years of consistent ordering history
- Regular demand pattern (not sporadic)
- Stable quantities (not wildly fluctuating)
- System tested multiple methods and found accurate predictions

**How to Use:**
- ✓ **Use forecast quantities directly** for purchase orders
- ✓ **Automate reordering** based on these forecasts
- ✓ Set safety stock at 1.0-1.2x forecast (minimal buffer)
- ✓ Review quarterly only

**Example:**
```
Part: ABC-123456
Tier: Tier_1_High
Confidence: High
Forecast: 25Q1=450, 25Q2=465, 25Q3=480

What This Means:
→ You can reliably order 450 units for Q1, 465 for Q2, 480 for Q3
→ Add 10-20% safety stock (45-90 units)
→ Total order: 495-540 units for Q1
```

**Typical Characteristics:**
- Confidence = "High"
- Quality = "Healthy"
- ≥8 quarters of history
- Model might be: Trend Analysis, Seasonal, Machine Learning

---

#### **Tier 2: MEDIUM CONFIDENCE** ⚠️ (Yellow Light - Use with Caution)

**What It Means:**
- 1-2 years of history (less data available), OR
- Sporadic ordering (20-40% of months had zero orders), OR
- Moderate variability in quantities

**How to Use:**
- ⚠️ **Use forecasts for direction**, not exact quantities
- ⚠️ Plan for a **range** (forecast ± 50%)
- ⚠️ Set safety stock at 1.5-2.0x forecast (larger buffer)
- ⚠️ Review monthly

**Example:**
```
Part: DEF-789012
Tier: Tier_2_Medium
Confidence: Medium
Forecast: 25Q1=85

What This Means:
→ Plan for 65-130 units (85 ± 50%)
→ Order based on upper range: 130 units
→ Monthly review to adjust if needed
→ Safety stock: 128-170 units (1.5-2x)
```

**Typical Characteristics:**
- Confidence = "Medium"
- Quality = "Intermittent" or 4-8 quarters of data
- Model might be: Croston's, Ensemble, Moving Average

---

#### **Tier 3: LOW CONFIDENCE** 📊 (Orange Light - Volume Only)

**What It Means:**
- <1 year of history, OR
- Highly sporadic (>40% zero orders), OR
- Wildly fluctuating quantities, OR
- System couldn't find accurate prediction method

**How to Use:**
- ⛔ **DO NOT use quarter-by-quarter forecasts**
- ✓ **Use annual total only** (sum all 4 quarters)
- ✓ Aggregate across similar parts for volume estimates
- ✓ Buyer/planner judgment overrides system
- ✓ Review weekly

**Example:**
```
Part: GHI-345678
Tier: Tier_3_Volume_Only
Confidence: Low (Review)
Forecast: 25Q1=12, 25Q2=12, 25Q3=12, 25Q4=12

What This Means:
→ DON'T order exactly 12 units each quarter
→ USE annual volume: ~48 units/year
→ Order based on: lead time + safety stock + buyer judgment
→ Flag for manual review before each order
```

**Typical Characteristics:**
- Confidence = "Low (Review)"
- Quality = "Sparse" or "Volatile"
- Model = "Fallback" or basic methods
- System shows: "Low (Review)" flag

---

#### **Tier 4: NOT FORECASTABLE** ❌ (Red Light - Manual Only)

**What It Means:**
- Dormant part (all zeros recently)
- <6 months of data
- One-time order (single spike in history)
- All forecasting methods rejected by quality checks

**How to Use:**
- ⛔ **Exclude from automated planning completely**
- ✓ **Order only when customer requests**
- ✓ Review for obsolescence
- ✓ Maintain minimal stock or zero stock

**Example:**
```
Part: JKL-901234
Tier: Tier_4_Not_Forecastable
Model: Fallback (All Gated)
Forecast: 25Q1=0, 25Q2=0

What This Means:
→ Remove from automated replenishment
→ Don't carry inventory unless customer pre-orders
→ Consider obsoleting this part number
→ Review annually only
```

**Typical Characteristics:**
- Model = "Fallback (All Gated)"
- All forecast quarters = 0
- Quality = "Sparse"
- System rejected all prediction methods

---

### **Quick Reference: How to Use Each Tier**

| Tier | Order Based On | Safety Stock Buffer | Who Approves | Review How Often |
|------|----------------|---------------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Tier 1: High** | Forecast quantities | 1.0-1.2x (10-20% extra) | System (automated) | Every 3 months |

## **Maintenance Event Integration** 🔧

**Problem:** Aircraft maintenance shutdowns reduce demand for certain parts. Forecasts should account for planned downtime.

**Solution:** System automatically adjusts forecasts when maintenance is scheduled.

### **How It Works:**

1. **Load Maintenance Schedule**
   - Input: CSV file with maintenance check names, start dates, end dates
   - Current system: 420 maintenance events loaded

2. **Match Events to Forecast Periods**
   - System identifies which quarters have overlapping maintenance
   - Example: "Plant Shutdown" from March 15 - April 30 affects Q2 2025

3. **Adjust Forecasts**
   - During maintenance quarters, demand is reduced by 30%
   - Example: Original forecast 100 units → Adjusted to 70 units

4. **Track Adjustments**
   - Output shows which events affected each forecast
   - Column: `Event_Applied` = "Plant Shutdown; Equipment Maintenance"

**Business Value:**
- Prevents over-ordering before maintenance periods
- Reduces excess inventory during aircraft downtime
- Aligns procurement with maintenance calendar

**Example:**
```
Part: MNO-567890 (Engine Component)
Normal Demand: 200 units/quarter
Forecast for Q2 2025: 140 units (↓ 30%)
Event Applied: "Annual Engine Overhaul"

Action: Order 140 units for Q2 instead of 200 → Save inventory costs
```

## **Business Intelligence Features** 📈

### **1. Demand Trend Detection (Momentum Analysis)**

**What It Does:** Automatically detects if demand is accelerating, slowing, or stable.

**How It Works:**
Compares three time periods:
- **Current**: Last quarter (most recent 3 months)
- **Mid-Term**: Last 6 months
- **Long-Term**: Last 12 months

**Status Categories:**

| Status | What It Means | Action Required |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **ACCELERATING** | Demand increasing steadily | Increase safety stock, validate with sales |
| **SLOWING** | Demand decreasing steadily | Review for obsolescence, reduce inventory |
| **REVERSAL (Recovery)** | Demand rebounding after decline | Watch closely, cautious restock |
| **REVERSAL (Downturn)** | Demand declining after growth | Reduce orders, clear excess stock |
| **STABLE** | Consistent demand pattern | Continue current planning |

**Example:**
```
Part: PQR-123789
Momentum: ACCELERATING
Last Quarter: 250 units
Last 6 Months Avg: 210 units
Last 12 Months Avg: 180 units

Action: Demand trending up 39% year-over-year
→ Increase safety stock from 50 to 75 units
→ Validate with sales team (new aircraft program?)
```

---

### **2. High Usage Alerts** ⚠️

**What It Does:** Flags parts where the forecast is significantly higher than historical average.

**Trigger:** Forecasted demand >50% above 12-month average

**Why It Matters:**
- Early warning for potential stockouts
- Catch unusual demand spikes
- Validate before committing to large orders

**Output:**
- `High_Usage_Alert = "YES (Check Volume)"` → Review required
- `High_Usage_Alert = "Normal"` → Proceed as usual

**Example:**
```
Part: STU-456123
12-Month Average: 200 units/quarter
Forecast Q1 2025: 320 units
Alert: YES (Check Volume)

Action Required:
→ Investigate: New aircraft order? Maintenance schedule change?
→ Confirm with sales/planning before ordering 320 units
→ If validated, adjust safety stock accordingly
```

---

### **3. Forecast Confidence Scoring** 🎯

**What It Does:** Tells you how reliable each forecast is.

**Confidence Levels:**

| Level | Meaning | Typical Error Range | How to Use |
|-------|---------|-------------------|------------|
| **High** | Reliable forecast | Within ±20% | Trust quantities, automate orders |
| **Medium** | Moderate uncertainty | Within ±20-50% | Use ranges, add buffer |
| **Low (Review)** | High uncertainty | >50% error | Manual review required |

**How System Calculates This:**
- Compares prediction error to historical average
- Lower error = Higher confidence
- Displayed clearly in `Confidence` column

**Example:**
```
Part: VWX-789456
Historical Average: 100 units/quarter
Prediction Error: 15 units
Confidence: High (15/100 = 15% error < 20% threshold)

Action: Trust this forecast for automated ordering
```

## **Special Cases in Aircraft Parts Forecasting** ✈️

### **Rotable Components**
- Parts that are repaired and returned to stock
- Demand includes: New purchases + Repair turnaround
- **Action:** Use forecast but adjust for expected repair returns

### **Consumable vs. Repairable**
- **Consumables**: Direct forecast usage (gaskets, seals, filters)
- **Repairables**: Forecast demand - expected repair returns

### **AOG (Aircraft On Ground) Parts**
- Critical parts that ground aircraft if unavailable
- **Action:** Even if Tier 2-3, maintain higher safety stock (3-5x forecast)
- Better to overstock than risk aircraft downtime

### **Life-Limited Parts (LLP)**
- Parts with mandatory replacement schedules
- Demand driven by flight hours + maintenance intervals
- **Action:** Cross-reference forecast with LLP tracking system

### **Seasonal Maintenance Patterns**
- Some parts only needed during annual heavy maintenance
- May show high zero% but demand is predictable
- **Action:** Check `Momentum_Status` and `Event_Applied` columns
- If shows seasonal pattern + maintenance events, forecast is reliable

## **Common Questions**

### **Q: Why does the system reject some forecasting methods?**
**A:** The 5 quality gates protect you from:
- Forecasts predicting 10x demand spikes (extreme values)
- Forecasts that jump wildly quarter-to-quarter (excessive volatility)
- Methods that were wrong by >100% during testing (poor fit)
- Forecasts predicting decline when part is clearly growing (trend mismatch)
- Forecasts dropping to zero for active parts (forecast collapse)

**Better to use historical average than an unrealistic forecast.**

---

### **Q: What does "Ensemble" mean in Model_Winner?**
**A:** When 2-3 methods are similarly accurate (within 15% error), the system **combines them** instead of picking just one. This is more reliable than trusting a single method.

Think of it like: "3 experts all think demand will be around 100 units, so let's average their opinions."

---

### **Q: Why does my forecast show "(floor)" after the number?**
**A:** The system applied a safety minimum. The forecasting method predicted lower, but business rules require a minimum quantity to prevent stockouts.

**Example:** Forecast shows "25Q1: 12 (floor)" means:
- Method predicted maybe 5-8 units
- System enforced minimum of 12 units (based on historical minimums)

---

### **Q: How do I know if a forecast is reliable?**
**A:** Check these 3 columns in order:
1. **Forecastability_Tier**: Tier_1_High = reliable, Tier_3/4 = not reliable
2. **Confidence**: High = reliable, Low = not reliable
3. **Model_Winner**: If it says "Fallback" or "Fallback (All Gated)" = not reliable

**Rule of Thumb:** Tier 1 + High Confidence = Trust it. Everything else = Use with caution or manual review.

---

### **Q: What should I do with "Tier_3_Volume_Only" parts?**
**A:** These parts have unpredictable quarter-by-quarter demand. Instead:
- Sum all 4 quarters to get annual volume
- Use this for capacity planning only
- Order based on: Current stock + lead time + buyer judgment
- Do NOT try to order exact quantities per quarter

---

### **Q: Why is my new part classified as "Sparse"?**
**A:** Parts with <4 quarters (1 year) of history don't have enough data for advanced forecasting. This is expected for new products.

**Action:** Use historical average or comparable part analysis until you have 4+ quarters of demand history.

---

### **Q: How does the system handle seasonal parts?**
**A:** If a part has a clear yearly pattern (e.g., only used in Q1), the advanced methods (Seasonal Analysis, Machine Learning) will detect this and forecast accordingly.

**Check:** `Momentum_Status` and quarterly forecast pattern. If Q1 forecast is much higher than Q2-Q4, system detected seasonality.

---

### **Q: What if the maintenance schedule changes?**
**A:** Re-run the system with updated events CSV file. The forecasts will automatically adjust to the new maintenance dates.

**Important:** Forecasts are only as accurate as the maintenance schedule you provide.

## **Appendix: Technical Details** 🔧

### **9 Forecasting Methods Used**

#### **Simple Methods** (Fast, Always Available)
1. **Naive**: Projects last quarter forward
2. **Moving Average**: Average of last 4 quarters
3. **Holt-Winters**: Trend detection with smoothing

#### **Advanced Statistical Methods** (For Healthy Parts)
4. **Theta**: Advanced pattern decomposition
5. **Seasonal ARIMA**: Captures yearly patterns
6. **Auto ARIMA**: Automatically tunes parameters
7. **Prophet**: Meta/Facebook method with event support

#### **Specialized Methods** (For Specific Patterns)
8. **Croston's**: Designed for sporadic/intermittent demand

#### **Machine Learning** (For Complex Patterns)
9. **XGBoost/LightGBM**: Learns from historical patterns and events

**Note:** System automatically selects which methods to try based on data quality. You don't need to understand these methods - the system handles it.

### **Quality Gate Details**

| Gate | Purpose | Threshold | Example |
|------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **1. Extreme Values** | Prevent unrealistic spikes/drops | >3x or <20% of average | Reject if forecasting 600 when typical is 100 |
| **2. Excessive Volatility** | Prevent erratic fluctuations | >2.5x historical variation | Reject if forecast varies ±200 when history varies ±50 |
| **3. Poor Fit** | Require minimum accuracy | Error <100% of average | Reject if error is 120 units when average is 100 |
| **4. Trend Mismatch** | Align with growth/decline | Match trend direction | Reject decline forecast when part growing 20%+ |
| **5. Forecast Collapse** | Prevent zero forecasts for active parts | >0 for parts with demand | Reject 0-2 unit forecast when average is 200 |

---

### **Forecast Floor Details**

| Floor Type | Calculation | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Historical** | 20% of lowest non-zero demand | Never go below past minimums | Min past order = 50 → Floor = 10 |
| **Moving Average** | 30% of last 4-quarter average | Stay connected to recent demand | Last 4Q avg = 80 → Floor = 24 |
| **Intermittent** | 10% of historical mean | Minimum for sporadic parts | Mean = 40 → Floor = 4 |
| **Absolute** | 1 unit | Always stock active parts | If mean >10 → Floor = 1 |

**Final Floor = Maximum of all 4 calculations**

---

### **Ensemble Blending Logic**

**When Multiple Methods Agree:**
- If top 2-3 methods have similar accuracy (within 15% error), combine them
- **Weight Calculation**: Methods with lower error get more influence
  - Weight = (1 / Error) / Sum of all (1 / Error)
- **Example:** Errors of 12, 14, 16 → Weights: 45%, 38%, 17%

**Why Combine Instead of Pick One:**
- Reduces risk of single-method failure
- More stable predictions
- Smooths out outliers
